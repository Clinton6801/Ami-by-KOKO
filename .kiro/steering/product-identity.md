# Product Identity & Characters

## App Name
**Àmì by Kòkò**

- **Àmì** (Yoruba: "sign" or "symbol") — the app name, named after the child guide
- **Kòkò** — the brand signature; the parrot mascot
- Always written with tone marks: **Àmì** not "Ami", **Kòkò** not "Koko"

## Characters

### Àmì
- 6-year-old Nigerian girl, curious and adventurous
- Warm, encouraging, never condescending
- Component: `src/components/characters/Ami.tsx`

### Kòkò (the parrot)
- The **audio avatar** — every phonics sound comes from Kòkò
- In Story Mode MVP: lost his voice; learning sounds restores it
- Component: `src/components/characters/Koko.tsx`

## Voice & Tone
- Speak to children directly: "Great job! Kòkò loves that sound!"
- Warm, celebratory, never punishing errors
- Simple vocabulary for ages 3–8
- UI copy feels like a friendly storybook

## Brand Colours
| Token | Hex | Use |

| `rose-500` | `#F43F5E` | Accent — celebration moments |

## Cultural Grounding Rules
- Every word-image association must include a Nigerian option:
  - "A is for Apple" AND "A is for Àgbàdo (corn)"
- Illustrations: Nigerian environments (markets, compounds, red soil, palm trees)
- Example names must be Nigerian: Tayo, Ngozi, Emeka, Hauwa
- Never use Western cultural references as primary
